\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

The rapid expansion of urban infrastructure necessitates the implementation of robust Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS) to mitigate traffic accidents and congestion. However, traditional vision-based monitoring systems often struggle to balance precise spatiotemporal detection of complex collisions with the stringent latency requirements inherent to real-time edge computing. To address this challenge, this study proposes a novel, hybrid vision-based framework that structurally separates spatial vehicle tracking from kinetic anomaly classification. The architecture utilises a pre-trained YOLO11 model and BoT-SORT for persistent spatial tracking, combined with Inverse Perspective Mapping (IPM) and Kalman filtering to estimate three-dimensional metric speeds. Simultaneously, an asynchronous kinetic thread captures unstructured physical motion employing Farnebäck dense optical flow, which feeds spatiotemporal tensors into a custom-trained ResNet-18 Convolutional Neural Network. The system's performance, evaluated against the CADP and TAD benchmark datasets, demonstrated an impressive overall accuracy of 96.61% and an F1-score of 0.966, successfully addressing class imbalance through the utilisation of a Focal Loss function. Importantly, the dual-threaded producer-consumer architecture maintained a real-time processing rate of 60 frames per second, with a Time-to-Accident latency of only 220 milliseconds. Additionally, the system incorporates automated GDPR-compliant obfuscation and mathematical camera stabilisation techniques, resulting in a highly resilient, privacy-preserving prototype suitable for deployment within modern municipal traffic surveillance networks.
